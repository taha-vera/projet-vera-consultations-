How to train AI on cultural data legally, privately, and with artist compensation.

VERA is an open protocol for aggregating cultural signals with differential privacy guarantees, no raw data retention, built-in artist remuneration, and full auditability.

The problem with AI training data today

Legal risk

GDPR restricts raw personal and cultural data usage. AI Act increases obligations on data provenance. Copyright blocks large-scale scraping.

No compensation

Artists and rights holders receive nothing when their work trains AI models. No mechanism exists to track or redistribute value.

No auditability

AI operators cannot prove their training data is legally compliant. Regulators cannot verify without accessing raw data.

Result: data is either illegal, inaccessible, or ethically contested.

What VERA solves

Legal AI training data pipelines

GDPR-compliant irreversible aggregation. Only statistical outputs leave the system. No raw data ever stored.

Artist compensation from AI usage

Usage-based redistribution model. 70% returns to the cultural ecosystem. SACEM and equivalent organizations distribute to artists.

Privacy-preserving audio streams

Differential privacy with Discrete Laplace mechanism. k-anonymity threshold enforced per contributor. Resistant to Mironov 2012 float attack.

Alternative to scraping

Only aggregated statistical patterns are exported. No individual signal is recoverable. Re-identification risk below 2.5%.

Core protocol invariants

Enforced by code, not policy. No one can override them.

  • Raw data is never stored or exposed
  • Outputs are irreversible statistical aggregates
  • Differential privacy enforced — ε budget tracked and bounded
  • k-anonymity threshold ≥ 100 enforced per contributor
  • Temporal decay — data has finite validity window (half-life 24h)
  • No identity reconstruction possible
  • External audit without raw data access

How it works

1. Ingestion

Audio and cultural streams enter the collection layer. Raw data never leaves this layer.

2. Aggregation

Signal extraction and noise injection. k-anonymity enforced before any output.

3. Privacy

Differential privacy with Discrete Laplace. Budget tracker enforces composition bounds.

4. Distribution

Statistical patterns exported to AI operators via SDK. No raw signal recoverable.

5. Remuneration

70% of revenue returns to cultural ecosystem. SACEM distributes to artists automatically.

Artists and rights holders
